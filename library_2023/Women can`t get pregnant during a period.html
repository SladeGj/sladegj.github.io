Women can`t get pregnant during a period
Although, this statement is really true, some people draw the wrong conclusion from it. Typically menstrual cycle length is between 28 and 30 days, so woman is usually fertile between days 11 and 21, although this can vary between cycles and from person to person. Previously, it was believed that healthy spermatozoa can remain active in the folds of the vagina for up to 5 days. However, new research has shown that some "long–livers" are able to stay there for up to 8 days. And although the probability that these single cells will then get inside the uterus, and then into the fallopian tube to merge with the egg, is very low, if pregnancy is undesirable for you, this fact must be taken into account.
