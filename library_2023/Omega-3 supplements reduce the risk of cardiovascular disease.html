Omega-3 supplements reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease

Previously, it was believed that fish oil helps keep the heart healthy, and it seemed logical. The myth relied on the fact that omega-3 fatty acids, which are found in fish oil, reduce the level of triglycerides and inflammation associated with an increased risk of cardiovascular diseases. But the study, which involved 12,500 people at high risk of heart and vascular disease who took omega-3 supplements daily, did not show a link between unsaturated fatty acids and protection from heart attack or stroke.
